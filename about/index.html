About the blog

I started blogging in 2012 on Google Blogger platform. That old blog is still alive, but not updated anymore. It contains several technical articles, but in general it’s not a technical blog.

In 2013 I bought the codedigging.com domain and started a self-hosted technical blog about reverse engineering of mobile applications. That blog was powered by Pelican and Disqus.

In 2017 I moved the technical blog to GitHub Pages and switched to Hugo. Now I use a simple shell script to generate HTML from Markdown with Hugo, and push the result to the repository with git. Very convenient and geeky way to blogging :)

Note! All header images for blog posts are from free photo stocks. If you recognise that some of the header images are under commercial license and can’t be used for free, please contact me, I’ll remove the images. ★

Please do not take the blog seriously :) Here I share my tutorials, talks and researches I did for fun. Unfortunately most of my commercial researches are private and can’t be shared because of agreements with the customers, so I usually do not post serious things here.
